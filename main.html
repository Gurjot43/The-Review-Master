Books

1. George Orwell's 1984 on Goodreads:

1984 is a powerful and disturbing novel that has earned its place as a classic. While some readers may find it slow or bleak, its themes remain frighteningly relevant in today's world. It's a book that will stay with you long after you finish reading it.

2. Lisa Halliday's Asymmetry:

"Asymmetry" is a thought-provoking read that delves into complex relationships and global issues. It's a strong debut for those who appreciate unconventional narratives and literary depth.

3. The Power of Habit

The book is well-written and engaging. Duhigg does a great job of making complex scientific concepts understandable to a lay audience. The stories are fascinating and help to illustrate the key points of the book.

4. Anissa Gray's The Care and Feeding of Ravenously Hungry Girls:

The Care and Feeding of Ravenously Hungry Girls is a powerful debut that explores themes of family, forgiveness, and the yearning for connection. With its well-drawn characters and unflinching portrayal of complex emotions, the novel is a captivating read for those who enjoy character-driven stories.

5. The Book of M by Peng Shepherd:

Peng Shepherd's "The Book of M" is a genre-bending novel that blends elements of dystopian fiction, fantasy, and even a touch of magical realism. Set in a world ravaged by a strange phenomenon that causes people to lose their shadows and memories, the story follows Ory and Max, a couple desperately trying to hold onto their love and each other.

6. To Kill a Mockingbird

Harper Lee's To Kill a Mockingbird is a captivating novel narrated by a young Scout Finch. Through Scout's innocent eyes, we witness the racial tensions of the Deep South as her lawyer father defends a wrongly accused Black man. This coming-of-age story tackles prejudice and justice with enduring themes, making it a must-read despite its slow start and limitations in perspective.

7. Pride And Prejudice by Jane Austen

Pride and Prejudice is a novel of manners, romance, and social critique. It subtly examines philosophical ideas through down-to-earth realism and humor. The story revolves around Elizabeth Bennet, the protagonist, and her interactions with the wealthy and enigmatic Mr. Darcy.

8. War And peace by Leo Tolstoe

War and Peace is often considered one of the greatest novels ever written. Its vast scope, intricate characters, and historical backdrop make it a literary masterpiece. The novel spans several decades, capturing the lives of aristocrats, soldiers, and peasants during the Napoleonic Wars.

9. Gitanjali by Rabindranath Tagore

Gitanjali (which translates to “Song Offerings”) is a collection of translated poems from Tagore’s original Bengali work. The English version includes 69 poems, capturing the essence of the Bengali collection

10. Midnight's Children by Salman Rushdie

Midnight's Children is a novel of epic proportions, blending realism with magical realism. The story follows Saleem Sinai, one of the “midnight children” born at the stroke of midnight on India’s independence day in 1947.
